# Paper Summary Instructions

Summarize the academic paper using the sections below. Write each section in clear prose unless noted otherwise. Assume the audience is a knowledgeable ML practitioner who has not read the paper. Keep each section focused and avoid padding.

---

## TL;DR
2–3 sentences capturing the paper's core contribution and why it matters. Write this last, after the full summary.

---

## Overview
- What problem does the paper address, and why is it important?
- What is the paper's main claim or hypothesis?
- What domain or application area does it target?
- What is the high-level approach taken?

---

## Innovation
- What is genuinely new in this work compared to prior approaches?
- Does it introduce a new method, architecture, loss function, training paradigm, or theoretical insight?
- Why is this innovation non-trivial?

---

## Related Work & Context
- What prior work does this paper build on?
- How does it differ from the closest alternatives?
- Where does it fit in the broader research landscape?

---

## Datasets
- What datasets are used for training and evaluation?
- Include size, domain, and any notable properties or preprocessing steps.
- Are the datasets public or proprietary?

---

## Architecture
- Describe the model architecture or system design at a conceptual level.
- Include key components, how they interact, and any novel design choices.
- Use bullet points for sub-components where helpful.

---

## Evaluation Methods
- What metrics are used to evaluate performance?
- What baselines does the paper compare against?
- Are there ablation studies? What do they test?

---

## Results
- What are the main quantitative results?
- Does the paper meet or exceed its claims?
- Highlight the most significant numbers relative to baselines.

---

## Limitations & Critiques
- What limitations do the authors acknowledge?
- What gaps or weaknesses are not addressed?
- Are there concerns about reproducibility, scope, or generalizability?

---

## Key Takeaways
3–5 bullet points summarizing the most important things to remember about this paper.
